Privacy Policy

This Privacy Policy explains how LuxRoute (“we”, “us”, “our”) collects, uses, and protects your information when you use our mobile applications, web application, and website (together, the “Services”).

1. Who we are

LuxRoute is a service route optimization and business management platform for service providers. LuxRoute is a product of NexumSpace Limited, registered in England & Wales.

2. Information we collect

2.1 Information you provide to us

We collect the information you provide directly, including:

  • Account details such as name, email address, and password.
  • Business details such as company name, service areas, customer information, and job data.
  • Communication content when you contact us (e.g. via contact forms, waitlist forms, or support).

2.2 Information collected automatically

When you use the Services, we may automatically collect:

  • Device and technical data (e.g. device type, operating system, browser type, IP address).
  • Usage data (e.g. pages viewed, features used, access times, referring pages).

2.3 Payment information

When you purchase a subscription, payments are processed securely by third‑party payment providers such as Stripe. We do not store your full payment card details on our servers. These providers may collect payment card information and billing details directly in accordance with their own privacy policies.

3. How we use your information

We use your information to:

  • Create and manage your account and provide the core LuxRoute services.
  • Optimize routes, manage schedules, jobs, and customer data as part of the service.
  • Process subscriptions and payments.
  • Communicate with you, including service notifications and responses to your enquiries.
  • Maintain and improve the performance, security, and reliability of the Services.
  • Comply with legal obligations and enforce our terms.

4. Legal bases for processing (EEA/UK users)

Where applicable (for example in the EEA or UK), we process your personal data on the following legal bases:

  • Performance of a contract – to provide and operate the Services you sign up for.
  • Legitimate interests – such as securing and improving our Services and preventing abuse.
  • Consent – for certain communications or optional features, where required by law.

5. How we share your information

We do not sell your personal data. We may share your information with:

  • Service providers / processors who help us operate the Services, such as: authentication and database providers (for example, Supabase), hosting providers, analytics or email service providers, and payment processors (for example, Stripe).
  • Business partners where needed to deliver features you use (for example, integrations you choose to enable).
  • Authorities or legal recipients when required to comply with law, legal process, or to protect rights, safety, and security.

These third parties are only allowed to process your personal data on our instructions and must protect it appropriately.

6. International transfers

Our Services may be provided using infrastructure located in different countries. As a result, your information may be transferred to and processed in countries outside of your own, which may have different data protection laws. Where required, we take appropriate safeguards (such as contractual protections) to help ensure your data remains protected.

7. Data retention

We retain your information for as long as it is reasonably necessary to provide the Services, comply with our legal obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce our agreements. When data is no longer needed, we will delete or anonymise it in line with our retention practices.

8. Your rights and choices

Depending on your location, you may have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you.
  • Request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Request deletion of your personal data, subject to legal obligations.
  • Object to or restrict certain processing, or withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.

To exercise these rights, please contact us using the details in the “Contact us” section below. We may need to verify your identity before fulfilling your request.

9. Children’s privacy

LuxRoute is not directed to children and is intended for use by businesses and adult users. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 (or under 16 where applicable). If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us so we can take appropriate action.

10. Security

We use reasonable technical and organisational measures to protect your information, including access controls, encryption in transit where appropriate, and regular security reviews. However, no system can be fully secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

11. Third‑party links and services

Our Services may contain links to third‑party websites or services. Their privacy practices are governed by their own policies, and we encourage you to review those policies before submitting personal information.

12. Changes to this Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. When we do, we will revise the “Last updated” date below. If we make material changes, we may provide additional notice (such as in‑app or on our website). Your continued use of the Services after an update means you accept the revised Policy.
